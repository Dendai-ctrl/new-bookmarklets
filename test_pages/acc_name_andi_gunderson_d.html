Forms notes and observations

  • ARIA-required="true"
    • ANDI shows aria-required="true" as "required".
    • Gunderson does not show aria-required. Would have to use second tool, WAF ARIA, to reveal aria-required true.
    • Required is a state property in Firefox. Not mapped to accessible name.
    • Similar state property shown by ANDI: readonly
  • Gunderson's shows ROLE. ANDI does not.
  • ANDI page shift
    • IE, ANDI forces the page to scroll to top when activated and keyboard focus goes to top.
    • Chrome, page and focus shift sometimes will happen. Sometimes the focus remains where it was before ANDI was activated
    • ** Would prefer no shift in content and no shift in focus when tool is activated.
  • ANDI effect on visual page
    • Chrome: Right side of content is cut off.
    • Chrome: Horizontal and vertical scroll bars were removed from the the page. Page up/down keys also don't work until focus is put in the page.
    • IE11: horizontal scroll bar removed. Vertical scroll bars remain.
  • Gunderson's bookmarklet:
    • Info is only revealed by mouseover and disappears when mouse is moved away. ** Would prefer that info is revealed when bookmarklet marker is selected and stays on screen until option to remove is selected/activated (like What Font? bookmarklet).
    • Sept 12, 2017 - Gunderson's favelets did not work on local file in IE. Worked on internet pages (www.justice.gov). This happened before in March 2016 and it magically worked again in June 2016. (Jon G. thinks it may be a change in IE since the bookmarklets have not been updated in Removing the IE results in for now.
    • For these 2 reasons, with no improvements to Gunderson's bookmarklet, I would recommend ANDI for forms.
  • Forms 18 to 22 are from Level access examples
  • Neither tool is able to show aria group labels on Open Ajax Accessibility Example 28 - Radiogroup
